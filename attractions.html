☆ Top Attractions & Food in NYC ☆

Statue of Liberty
An iconic symbol of freedom, the Statue of Liberty is a must-see landmark. You can visit Liberty Island and enjoy amazing views of Manhattan.
Location on Google Maps | Official Website
Central Park
A huge green oasis in the middle of Manhattan, perfect for walking, biking, and relaxing. Famous for its lakes, bridges, and open spaces.
Location on Google Maps
Brooklyn Bridge
One of the oldest suspension bridges in the U.S., connecting Manhattan and Brooklyn. Great for walking and skyline views.
Location on Google Maps
Empire State Building
A world-famous skyscraper with observation decks offering breathtaking views of the city skyline.
Location on Google Maps | Official Website
Times Square
The vibrant heart of NYC, filled with neon lights, theaters, shops, and endless energy. A must-see for first-time visitors.
Location on Google Maps
The New York Times Building
Home of one of the world’s most famous newspapers. Iconic modern architecture in Midtown Manhattan.
Location on Google Maps | Official Website
The Metropolitan Museum of Art (The Met)
One of the world's largest art museums, featuring thousands of artworks from around the globe, from ancient to modern times.
Location on Google Maps | Official Website
New York University (NYU)
One of the leading private universities in the U.S., located in Greenwich Village. Known for its vibrant campus life and beautiful urban setting.
Location on Google Maps | Official Website
American Museum of Natural History
A world-famous museum showcasing dinosaurs, space exhibits, and natural wonders. Perfect for science and history lovers.
Location on Google Maps | Official Website
MoMA – Museum of Modern Art
Home to some of the world’s most famous modern and contemporary artworks, including pieces by Van Gogh, Picasso, and Warhol.
Location on Google Maps | Official Website
